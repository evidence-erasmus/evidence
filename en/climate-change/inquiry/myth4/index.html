Scientific Source: Conducting an experiment

Means and materials:

  1. Sand or soil
  2. Water
  3. Large plastic bottles cut in half
  4. Plastic cups
  5. Ice cubes
  6. Transparent film
  7. Light bulb
  8. Marker

Experimental procedure:

  1. Place an equal amount of sand or soil in the two large plastic bottles.
  2. Place a plastic cup with the spout in the soil of each plastic bottle and secure the other plastic cup over the 1st glass with the spout facing up. Place the same number of ice in every 2nd plastic cup.
  3. Make grooves between the bottom two parts of the plastic cups so that water can flow from the ice into each plastic bottle.
  4. Put an equal amount of water in each plastic bottle.
  5. Cover each plastic bottle with cling film.
  6. Turn on the light and make observations after 20 minutes.

Comments

Cherry picking:
This technique is defined as “selectively chooses data leading to a desired conclusion that differs from the conclusion arising from all the available data”.

Jumping to conclusions:
Ignorance of information and arbitrary conclusion.

Impossible expectations:
This involves demanding unrealistic levels of proof, or misrepresenting the nature of scientific uncertainty. As science is typically probabilistic, calls for absolute scientific certainty are an effective method of casting doubt on scientific findings. This denialist technique is known as “Scientific Certainty Argumentation Methods”.

Data simplification.
